CHAMPIONSHIP FAQ

Special Stages are individual closed tracks (gravel paths, ice layers, or tarmac mountain roads) where pilots push vehicles to maximum limits to record the lowest time.

Rally1 setups integrate a standard 1.6L internal turbo combustion block alongside an efficient 100kW electric system to generate brief bursts over 500 horsepower.

Pace notes are highly detailed codes describing corners, severity angles, crest jumps, and surface changes. The co-driver reads these continuously so the driver can handle blind bends at high speeds.

Mechanics receive strict, timed windows (usually 15, 30, or 45 minutes) to completely repair body panels, swap gearboxes, rebuild suspensions, or dial in setups for the next loop.
